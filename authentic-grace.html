Frontend E-commerce Website

As a group project of Moms Can Code School, we built and deployed a frontend e-commerce website for a hypothetical brand called Authentic Grace.


Project Challenge

Our assignment was to design and develop a minimal viable product for a frontend e-commerce website for a hypothetical client, Authentic Grace. We were required to focus on the implementation of the website, how to create the sections of the website and incorporate the features needed by the client. Details of the client, buyer persona and needed features are described below:

  • Brand Name: Authentic Grace, a small family owned business in Charleston, SC
  • What We Sell: We have a fun and creative boutique selling women’s clothing
  • Buyer persona: Erin, 37, a fit and active woman with two children under the age of 6. She loves to add pieces to her wardrobe that won’t break the bank and are easily washed. She has a classic style and likes to mix in a few trendy pieces. At this time she is a stay at home Mom but she still wants to look stylish and not get stuck in the Mom.
  • Required Features:
    1. Straightforward logical web structure
    2. Consistent Theme
    3. Images
    4. Social media links
    5. Footer
    6. Contact info for the store
    7. Customer testimonials

Our Approach and Solution

We first discussed how Authentic Grace can be unique in its offerings and be able to win the mind share of the target audience, whom we identified as a busy working mom who rarely has time to go out for clothes shopping. We decided that personalization, the ease of shopping from home and giving confidence to working moms are important qualities of this brand's website. Using this as our starting point, we came up with the value proposition, theme color and also the idea of providing handpicked clothes tailored to the customer's taste and needs.

After building the wireframe in Figma, we decided to split the work by diving the website into multiple sections. Therefore all of us took part in programming the website with HTML, CSS and JavaScript. We collaborated and communicated through GitHub, Slack and Whereby. Our completed frontend website was deployed using Netlify.
